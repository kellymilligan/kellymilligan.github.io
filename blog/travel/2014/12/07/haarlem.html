Haarlem

Dec 7, 2014

Haarlem is a city to the west of Amsterdam, only 20m by train! It’s significantly smaller, but it’s so close that many Haarlem residents commute to Amsterdam each day.

Haarlem is a lovely little city. It’s full of gorgeous old buildings, canals, churches, markets, boutique shops and more. It’s got a bunch of the great things about Amsterdam, without the overwhelming number of tourists and Chamsterdamy souvenirs (seriously, they’re awful).

The city surrounds an amazing, massive, and ancient church called the Grote Kerk. On Saturdays in the town square (right beside the church), there’s a food, farmers and goods market where you can get some choice stuff.

It’s a highly cultural city with loads of art galleries and museums. One I visited is the Teylers Museum, and it houses an enormous collection of fossils, as well as a bunch of historical electric and medical science curios. Lot’s of fascinating stuff!

I also visited the Jopen brewery, but I’ll split that into a separate beer-themed post.

I just did a day trip when I took these photos, but I’ve also been back to go to a music gig at Patronaat since then.
