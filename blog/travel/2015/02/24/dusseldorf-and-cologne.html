Düsseldorf + Altbier, Cologne + Kölsch

Feb 24, 2015

Oh, Germany… You sure love beer and sausage.

I had a cool weekend trip to these 2 small, western German cities a few weeks back. There’s so much history here, and I can’t wait to explore more. Cologne (Köln) has been a Roman settlement for more than 1000 years. They’re both pretty small, and both quite traditional cities. I’m really looking forward to checking out Berlin which is hear is much more similar to Amsterdam in culture.

The beer is great, and has so much traditional charm. It’s worth going to both just to check out their local historical beer culture, Altbier for Düsseldorf and Kölsch for Köln. It comes around in big trays full of 200ml glasses, they’ll give you one, make a pencil mark on your coaster, then move on. Once you’re done, drop your coaster on top of your empty glass, and pay for the lot before you leave. Beer is cheap, about €1,20 to €1,50, so after several beers you end up shocked at a less than €10 bill :) I personally prefer Altbier, but both are excellent styles. Over the years it seems some of the brands have become pretty macro, but the standouts are phenomenal beers.

The Dom towers and church in Köln are immense, stunning, and outrageous. We had a pretty heavy snow fall most of the day in Köln, which added to the wintery and quiet atmosphere of the city. Düsseldorf is much more modern, with some interesting architecture kicking around.
