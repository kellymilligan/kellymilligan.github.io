Code, beer, and limited legroom

In Bruges

Mar 15, 2015

Decided to do another trip to Belgium, this time Bruges (or Brugge). It’s a unique experience for sure! I wonder how quiet this city must’ve been before that movie came out… because now it’s crazy busy with tourists (including me)!

It’s extremely tidy, quaint and charming. Every house is made of old brick, with perfectly painted doors in nulled colour schemes. You’ll often find yourself being the only one walking down a narrow side-street. It’s crazy for taking photos, because every corner you turn presents you with another charming, photo-worthy view. Apologies, but for this reason some of below photos can be quite repetitive!

The famous Bellfry is a fun experience and offers great views over the city. That staircase gets pretty narrow near the top!

Good food can be tricky to find, but good beer (mostly Belgian) is everywhere. Being such a tourism driven town prices are high. Cafe Rose Red is definitely worth a visit for some great beers and lovely Charcuterie, and the hottest freakin mustard I’ve ever tasted. What’s awesome is that they offer Orval in young, 6+month and 12+month options! De Garre is a hidden gem, and hard to find down a narrow side-alley. Excellent tripel with hints of cherry/almond essence and served with a little bit of cheese on the side. I did some other beer related activities like visiting Brouwerij De Halve Mann, but nothing that I’d go out of my way to do again.

Around 7pm on Saturday night they cordoned off the Markt square. Apparently the car you can see in the pictures may have contained a bomb! After I got out of De Garre around an hour later everything was open again so here’s hoping it was merely speculation.

I found only 1 bottled beer that was cheapear in Bruges than in Amsterdam, Tilquin Quetsche, which is a simply fantastic fruit lambic (purple plums) and one of my absolute favourite sour beers. I grabbed 3 to bring home at €7 a pop, about 2/3 the price I can find it for here. Other stuff like all of the trappist beers, Cantillon and 3 Fonteinen are significantly more expensive.

I’ll finish by saying that you really only need 1 full day to explore Bruges, and I ended up grabbing the train back early at around 2pm on the Sunday.
